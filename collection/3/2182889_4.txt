´Chamber´ is an extremely old word, barely used today except in legal or political contexts to describe a room for legal or political work. . . e.g.´the chamber of commons´ (British politics). . Chamber was used in olden times for usually a lady´s private room hence the old nursery rhyme:. . "Goosey, Goosey Gander, where do you wander?. Upstairs and downstairs and in my lady´s chamber". . A chamber can also mean a very large empty room for other purposes depending on very specific contexts. eg 'gas chamber' 'chamber of horrors'. . Room is a general word meant for any individual space in a house or building, not necessarily a bedroom.
