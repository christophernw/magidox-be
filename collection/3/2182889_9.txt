Chamber ;. . 1) a hall in a public building that is used for formal meetings - the Senate/House chamber. 2) one of the parts of a parliament - Lower/Upper Chamber. 3) (in compounds) a room used for the particular purpose that is mentioned - a burial chamber. 4) an enclosed space in teh body, in a plant or in a machine - the chambers of the heart. 5)the enclosed space under the ground - a vast underground chamber. 6) (old use) a bedroom or private room. . Room ;. . 1)(in building) a part of a building that has its own walls, floor and ceiling and is usually used for a particular purpose - a dining/sitting/living room. 2) (in adjectives) having the number of rooms mentioned - a three-roomed. 3) (in hotel) a bedroom - a double/single room. 4) (place to live - old-fashioned British English) a set of two or more rooms that you rent to live in. Synonymous to "lodgings". 5) (space) empty space that can be used for a particular purpose - legromm, standing room and etc.. 6) (possibility) the possibility of something existing or happening / the opportunity to do something - no room for doubt. 7) (people) all people in a room
