The major reason was the economic interests of the rural areas of America were the opposite of the urban interests. For example a farmer wants to receive the highest price for his wheat, but the urban dweller wants the lowest price for a loaf of bread. The Populists under Presidential Candidates James Weaver in 1892 and WJ Bryan (also Democratic candidate) in 1896,1900 and 1908  failed to relate to urban America, much as Governor Al Smith in 1928 failed to catch on in America's farmlands.
