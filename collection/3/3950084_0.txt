Your company will no longer be matching in your 401K but if your new company has a 401K, that would be a good place to roll your money.  If they don't have a 401K plan and you take the money, unless you are 59 and 1/2 or meet one of the few other exceptions, you will pay a 10% penalty in addition to the income tax for an early withdrawal.  ALso, the age at which you can draw full sociail security benefits is increasing. Social security was never designed to to be the sole source of income in retirement so you need to have your own retirement income to supplement what you will receive.
