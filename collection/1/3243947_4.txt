Having a specified gait. Often used in combination: smooth-gaited; slow-gaited. . . Gait . NOUN: . . 1. A particular way or manner of moving on foot: a person who ran with a clumsy, hobbling gait..  . 2.Any of the ways, such as a canter, trot, or walk, by which a horse can move by lifting the feet in different order or rhythm..  . 3.Rate or manner of proceeding: The project went forward at a steady gait..  . TRANSITIVE VERB: . gait·ed , gait·ing , gaits . To train (a horse) in a particular gait or gaits.
