Pyrrhic victory &#92;PIR-ik&#92;, noun:. A victory achieved at great or excessive cost; a ruinous victory. . Technically it was a victory for the British, who attacked the patriot fortifications -- but a Pyrrhic victory if ever there was: out of 2,200 British soldiers 1,034 were killed or wounded, including one in nine of all the officers the British lost in the whole war.. -- Geoffrey Wheatcroft, "A Revolutionary Itinerary", The Atlantic, April 2001
