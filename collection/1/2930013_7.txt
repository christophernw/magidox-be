Mostly this is historical and cultural. The black cat superstition comes partly from the association of the colour black with death and partly with the association of cats in general as witches' familiars and with black cats as the personification of eveil. However, this is culturally-determined and seems to be particularly Anglo-Saxon in origin. Indeed, where I come from (North Wales) the black cat is considered lucky.. . The bad luck associated with the breaking of mirrors comes from the expense involved in buying the first true silver-backed mirrors. These were originally extremely expensive items and the initial bad luck was the monetary cost. Of course, this soon evolved into a myth so that the breaking of mirrors in general became a matter of bad luck
