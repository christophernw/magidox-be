"The first-pass effect (or first-pass metabolism) is a phenomenon of drug metabolism.. . After a drug is swallowed, it is absorbed by the digestive system and enters the portal circulation. The absorbed drug is carried through the portal vein into the liver.. . The liver is responsible for metabolizing many drugs. Some drugs are so extensively metabolized by the liver that only a small amount of unchanged drug may enter the systemic circulation, so the bioavailability of the drug is reduced.. . Alternative routes of administration (e.g., intravenous, intramuscular, sublingual) avoid the first-pass effect."
