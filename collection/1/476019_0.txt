Carrots are uniformly orange and have a large amount of beta-carotene found throughout the entire root, right to the external layer.. Oranges, on the other hand, also contain beta-carotene, but mostly in the juice, with just a little on the outside layer of the orange peel (even when ripe).. The beta-carotene is a precursor to Vitamin A and do to the large number of conjugated double bonds is orange in color.
