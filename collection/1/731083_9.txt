Choosing A Pineapple. . Pineapples do not ripen and get sweeter after they're picked from the plant they grow on, so it's important to choose a good pineapple at the grocery store. According to the Hawaii Department of Agriculture, here's what you should look for:. . Select a fragrant, heavy, symmetrical pineapple with crisp green leaves, not dry, yellow ones. . Avoid pineapples with soft spots, watery "eyes", or discoloration. . Pick a pineapple whose shell is golden about halfway up--the gold color starts at the bottom and moves toward the top. . . I prefer the hybrid "Super Sweet' or "Golden" varieties. When choosing a hybrid pineapple, my experience is that those with the sweetest, most fragrant smell tend to taste the sweetest.
