Well this is a very good but also tricky question!. . Well, you see For is usually followed by a word or name " Is that enough FOR you?, That is grammar For you! I bought this FOR my wife! If it wasn't FOR Brian, we would never have known! ( I think you get the picture!)". . Whereas In order to is usually  followed by a verb" In order to GET their attention..., I will have to WAIT in order to BE able to talk to him. ( You get the picture)". . So that is the real difference between the two!. . You don't have to thank me it was my pleasure!
