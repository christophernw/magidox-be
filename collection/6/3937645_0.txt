Malaysia-Singapore lie outside the Pacific Ring of Fire - a zone of frequent earthquakes and volcanic eruptions that encircles the basin of the Pacific Ocean, and is shaped like a 40,000 km long horseshoe. It is associated with a nearly continuous series of oceanic trenches, island arcs, and volcanic mountain ranges and/or plate movements. 90% of the world's earthquakes and 81% of the world's largest earthquakes occur along the Ring of Fire. . . Indonesia lies between the Ring of Fire along the northeastern islands adjacent to and including New Guinea and the Alpide belt along the south and west from Sumatra, Java, Bali, Flores, and Timor.
