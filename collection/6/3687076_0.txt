Almost invariably, Biblical scholars are professors at universities, which involves lots of research and publication as well as teaching.. . Biblical scholars with strong particular faith committments and denominational affiliation often teach at church-affiliated institutions (Bible colleges, denominational colleges, seminaries and the like) in departments of Theology.. . Biblical scholars with no particular faith commitment or affiliation usually teach in non-church-affiliated public or private universities or colleges (state universities, private liberal arts colleges, and the like) in departments of comparative religion or religious studies.. . Sometimes Biblical scholars are historians, sometimes they are archaeologists, sometimes they are experts in literary criticism.
