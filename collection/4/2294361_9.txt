Valley, southeastern California, U.S.. . The lowest, hottest, driest portion of North America, it is about 140 mi (225 km) long and 5–15 mi (8–24 km) wide. The Amargosa River flows into it from the south and contains a small pool, Badwater, that at 282 ft (86 m) below sea level is the lowest point in North America. Death Valley was formerlyan obstacle to pioneer settlers (hence its name); it later was a centre of borax mining.
