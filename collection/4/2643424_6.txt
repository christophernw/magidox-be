Mardi Gras  . Definition: Before the sacrifices of Lent, people have traditionally held celebrations to let off steam and use up food that had to be given up for Lent. . . The English call this period Shorvetide refering to being shirven, or making confession, in church. . . Mardi Gras is French for fat Tuesday. It refers to the day you must give up cooking fats before giving them up for Lent. . . Russians call the week before Ash Wednesday Butter Week in reference to giving up butter. . . Germans hold festivals called Fasching or Karneval. The Polish call it Zapusty, Hungarians call it Farsang, and Greeks call the final week before Lent Apokries/
