Limited how?  It has value as long as it is known to be anecdotal.  That means that this person saw it happen 'once upon a time'.  An anecdote is a story.  There is nothing wrong with making a decision based upon anecdotal evidence if it the kind of decision where one occurrence is enough.  In some companies, one occurrence of something is enough to prompt a company wide rule.. . Scientifically, anecdotal implies outside the laboratory or outside of a controlled experiment.  If you do not know what the control factors were or the conditions surrounding some interesting occurrence, it is very difficult to repeat and of little value as hard evidence in proving a scientific theory.
