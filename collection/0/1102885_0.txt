First off...Men do not have a "cherry". . Second, the "cherry" is actually called the hymen and is a thin layer of tissue theat covers the vaginal canal. Most women lose this prior to sexual activity through the use of tampons or medical exam. Others have it "popped" during first sexual encounter. It is sometimes mildly pinful and can bleed a bit.. . In earlier times the hymen was closely guarded by a girls parents, as it was a sure sign of virginity.
