con·straint (kən-strānt') n.. .    1. The threat or use of force to prevent, restrict, or dictate the action or thought of others..    2. The state of being restricted or confined within prescribed bounds: soon tired of the constraint of military life..    3. One that restricts, limits, or regulates; a check: ignored all moral constraints in his pursuit of success..    4. Embarrassed reserve or reticence; awkwardness: “All constraint had vanished between the two, and they began to talk” (Edith Wharton).. . [Middle English constreinte, from Old French, from feminine past participle of constraindre, to constrain. See constrain.]
