Most religions are extremely protective of their identity, so there are things built into them to ensure that they won't get watered down through cultural mixing and intermarriage.  For Christians it is the concept of original sin.  The Jewish faith shuns a man who marries a woman from outside the religion, the Hindu's used to cling strongly to the caste system.  Muslims have a strong distaste for infadels.. . The irony is that this exclusion of other faiths is one of the common principles you are talking about in your question!
