Since benzene consists of a ring of six carbons having covalent bonds so as to form a hexagon shape, and each carbon has a valence of four (you can think of it as having four "arms" each of which needs to be holding on to something), the convention is to draw benzene with alternating single and double bonds to graphically illustrate that all bonds are satisfied.. . What is really happening is better modelled using "resonance theory".  Here's a link to a page about it, this is probably more than you wanted to know!
