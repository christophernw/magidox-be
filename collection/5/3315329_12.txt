A Knob of Butter .   . . . Often in cooking it is required to have on hand a knob of butter. This may be for new potatoes, corn on the cob or many other culinary delights.. . The problem though is this:. How big is a knob?. . This question had long been lacking a true mathematical definition.. . A 'Knob of Butter' is defined thus:. An isosceles right-angled tetrahedron whose adjacent is equal to half the height of the block of butter
