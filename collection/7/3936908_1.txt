A school is most commonly a place designated for learning. The range of institutions covered by the term varies from country to country.. . In many countries, including the United States and United Kingdom, a school may also be a partially autonomous or entirely separate institution (not necessarily a part of a system of compulsory public education at all) dedicated to learning within one particular field, such as a school of economics (e.g. the London School of Economics), a school of dance, or a school of journalism.. .
